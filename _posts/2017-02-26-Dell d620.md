## Inconsistency

For those who didn't _know_, I'm back in **CHILE** and I think it's a good time to get back to writing — technically in Spanish since this post is mostly just a quick update, short and informative.

About 5 years ago I installed [UBUNTU](https://www.ubuntu.com/) on a [Notebook](http://www.dell.com/us/dfb/p/latitude-d620/pd) that was lying around the house, you know, **to turn it into a media center** — and the best option for that has always been **Ubuntu**.

As always, installs are _pretty straight forward_, no drama, well... except that it didn't recognize the WIFI, like the driver.

> I'll fix it later

Yeah, a lot later.

So, _today_ was finally the day. After spending 6 hours fighting with **GRUB** installation issues I realized the problem wasn't the Linux distro — talking about the wifi — and I discovered **that it's a total pain in the ass** to sort that out. I think it's worth leaving you the link to the solution:

[The lovely link](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/Driver/bcm43xx)

**bonus track**:

If you're installing from a **USB** like I did and you don't have access to the drive, you'll need to **decompress the ISO** to get to the famous **B43** and copy it to the computer. Other than that, you shouldn't have any issues.

_right now I'm enjoying copying the backed-up files back to the notebook_
