## On games, jailbreaking, and how I hope Nintendo revives Metroid.

Welcome to another _pointless post_. Today I'm going to talk about how I switched from a **PSVITA** to a **New 3DS** (worst name Nintendo ever came up with) — and honestly I can sum it up in 2 lines: **PSVITA has no games**. A few years ago [SONY](http://gamingbolt.com/sony-confirms-end-of-ps-vita-support-officially-labels-it-legacy-platform) decided to stop giving the console **AAA** game support and turn it into an _indie_-only platform.

Fair enough, I think I've played more good _indies_ than **AAA** games. Remember that an example of a **AAA** game is [CALL OF DUTY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?time_continue=17&v=5E82ZkHTiVU), which is the same thing every year. The problem is that the _indie support_ was just barely there — games like [NUCLEAR THRONE](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yNb9CDXUpY0), which require a steady FPS, couldn't deliver. It ran disgustingly, unplayably badly. Updates took ages. I sold the console before the bug fix ever came out.

The catalog itself wasn't terrible, but a big chunk of it was **JRPG** games, and honestly I get bored of those super-sexualized games with barely-clothed schoolgirls and the same characters over and over, or games about shooting or ripping clothes off **lolis**.

## Hello nintendo my old friend

In my search for portability, my only real option was the **Nintendo 3DS**. Sure, the **PSP** catalog has always been bigger, more varied, and infinitely more emulation-friendly, but my need to play online made me trade in my Sony paperweight for what feels like a toy for 5-year-olds. One of the things that bugged me most about the **3DS** was its super infantilized interface — it's well made, it's just a console designed for kids. Mine I actually bought used from a kid (anyone under 20 is a kid in my book).

Obviously before making the switch I checked the **3DS SCENE**. Oh sweet hackers, the console is already fully unlocked — _remember kids, piracy is bad_. Since at that point I was in Canada and didn't have the means to **unlock, jailbreak** it myself, I let an Asian friend do it. Once back in Chile I did it again myself — straightforward but takes a while.

**Monster Hunter** YEAH! Finally playing one of my favorite games online. _WAIT, THERE'S A METROID PRIME?_!!

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/weiqzen1trs" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe>

WHAT A GAME! I had already played both **GameCube** ones and more than half of **Metroid Prime Hunters** on the Nintendo **DS**. I knew the **3DS** game was rough, but how rough can a **Metroid** be when the last handheld one was over 10 years ago and it was excellent...

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/RkUMotJb8mk" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe>

The video says it all. After that disappointment came the obvious question: can I play **DS** games on the **3DS**?

### DS into 3DS

If we're going the legal route, yes you can — just pop in the cartridge and boom, **DS into 3DS**. Of course in Chile there aren't many **Metroid Prime Hunters** cartridges floating around, and their prices are a bit outrageous for a game that's almost 10 years old — and I'm not even talking collector's condition (eternal love for the little boxes). Back to the **SCENE**.

This is where things get weird. The way **DS** games are programmed makes it nearly impossible to emulate them on a **3DS**. WHY? My friend [TCPixel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC2rtMQ5JcVDnv-ooN5BblIA) has a quick explanation.

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/uDMdFf1xte0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe>

The thing is that yesterday I tried [TWloader](https://github.com/Robz8/TWLoader/wiki/Installation-and-upgrade) and it works pretty well, considering how complex those *hard-coded* little cards are. There's still no save support, and **Metroid Prime Hunters** takes about 4 minutes to load with 40 seconds for each cutscene — but there's hope, friends.
